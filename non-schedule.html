Important before you start

This page will tell you about the most effective exercises for beginners. so if you a beginner start with these exercises and do the most number or time (if the exercise is specified with time to finish it) and day after day you can go on and increment your highest number and once you feel you want to challenge yourself and draw a particular plan just drawn for you, you can always draw you own train from the home page.

We all know that physical activities is good for our health, but not just that, it's good for our feelings and our emotions too.

So here in this page I am going to explain to you some of the best and most effective exercises for beginners, and these exercises are:

  1. 1- Pushups (start with 10 in every set)

    Pushups are a type of bodyweight exercise that targets the muscles in your chest, shoulders, and triceps. Some specific benefits of pushups include:

    1. Improved upper body strength
    2. Enhanced stability
    3. Better posture
    4. Increased muscle endurance
    5. Increased cardiovascular fitness

  2. 2- Bridge (start with 20 in every set)

    The bridge exercise is a type of strength training exercise that targets the muscles in your glutes, hamstrings, and lower back. Some specific benefits of the bridge exercise include:

    1. Improved lower body strength
    2. Improved flexibility
    3. Increased mobility
    4. Better athletic performance

  3. 3- Lunges (start with 20 in every set)

    Challenging your balance is an essential part of a well-rounded exercise routine.Lunges do just that, promoting functional movement while also increasing strength in your legs and glutes.

  4. 4- Squats (start with 20 in every set)

    Squats enhance leg strength and can improve stability, flexibility, mobility, athletic performance, bone density, and posture.

  5. 5- Plank (start with 45 seconds in every set)

    Planks are a bodyweight exercise that target the muscles in the core and can improve core strength, stability, posture, muscle endurance, and athletic performance. Planks involve holding a static position supported by the hands or forearms and require balance and core engagement to maintain proper form. Planks are a useful exercise for strengthening the muscles of the core, which play a vital role in supporting the body and maintaining proper posture.

  6. 6- Plank to Downward Dog (start with 20 in every set)

    This move will test your upper body, especially your shoulders. Who says you need weights for a shoulder workout?

  7. 7- Sit-ups (start with 15 in every set)

    Sit-up exercises are a type of strength training exercise that target the muscles of the abdomen and hip flexors. They are often used to improve core strength and stability, as well as to help tone and define the muscles of the abdomen. Sit-ups can also help to improve posture and reduce the risk of back pain.
